What is a Tight End (TE)?

On a Football team, a Tight End is an offensive player whose role is to either catch and run with the football downfield towards the end zone or block the opposing team's defense, depending on the play. With as many as three tight ends on the field at one time, these players will line up on the line of scrimmage or in the backfield near the quarterback. You must have one active tight end playing on your Fantasy Football team; however, you can also choose to use a tight end in your flex position.

How does a Tight End earn Fantasy Football points?

Given the tight end's dual role of blocking and receiving during the game depending on the play, these players typically have the lowest scoring potential of the offensive line. Each time a tight end completes a play during the game, the player will earn positive, negative, or no points for your Fantasy Football team. Positive points are awarded based on the number of rushing yards earned, touchdowns scored, the number of yards earned by completed catches, and more. Points are taken away from your Fantasy Football team for this position when negative yards are gained because the opposing team tackles the player behind the line of scrimmage. No points are awarded if the tight end completes a defensive play or does not successfully catch the ball.
