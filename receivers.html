What is a Wide Receiver (WR)?

On a Football team, a Wide Receiver is the player on the team's offense whose role is to catch the football when it is thrown by the quarterback, and when possible, run the ball towards the end zone. Typically, the wide receiver will line up on the line of scrimmage on either side of the offensive line and will run downfield around the opposing team's defensive players to catch a pass. You must have at least two active wide receivers playing on your Fantasy Football team at one time; however, you can also choose to use a wide receiver in your flex position.

How does a Wide Receiver earn Fantasy Football points?

Each time a wide receiver completes a play during the game, the player will earn positive, negative, or no points for your Fantasy Football team. Positive points are awarded based on the number of yards earned by completed catches, touchdowns scored, rushing yards gained in the direction of the end zone after catching the ball, and more. Points are taken away from your Fantasy Football team for this position when negative yards are gained as a result the completed catch wehn the opposing team tackles the player behind the line of scrimmage. No points are awarded if the quarterback targets the wide receiver and he does not catch the ball.
